Safety Tips 🛡️

From fitting your helmet to what to do if you witness a crash — here's everything you need to know.

⛑️ Helmet Fitting Guide
A helmet only protects you if it fits right
✌️

Two Finger Rule

Two fingers between your eyebrows and the helmet edge. More than that — too loose.

✅

The V Check

Straps form a "V" right below each ear. Adjust until they meet just under your earlobe.

👄

Chin Buckle

Only ONE finger between the strap and your chin. Snug but comfortable.

🚫

Loose = Dangerous

A loose helmet can fly off in a crash — almost as dangerous as wearing none at all.

🛣️ Road Safety Rules
Know the rules before you ride
🚦

Ride With Traffic

Always ride in the same direction as cars. Riding against traffic is one of the most dangerous things a cyclist can do.

🤚

Hand Signals

Left: arm out. Right: left arm bent up or right arm out. Stop: left arm bent down.

💡

Be Visible

White front light, red rear light after dark. Bright clothing. Never assume drivers see you.

🛤️

Sidewalk Rules

In Suffolk County, e-bikes and scooters are fully banned from sidewalks. Check local rules for regular bikes.

🚨 If YOU Get In An Accident
Stay calm — here's exactly what to do
😮‍💨

Stay Calm

Take a breath. Panic makes everything worse. Assess yourself first.

📵

Don't Move If Hurt

Head/neck/back pain? Stay still. Moving could make a spinal injury worse.

📞

Call 911

If injured or another person involved, call immediately. Don't assume it's too minor.

📸

Document Everything

Photos of the scene, injuries, vehicles involved. Exchange contact info.

🆘 If You Come Upon An Accident
You don't need to be a doctor — just knowing this could save a life
👀

Check For Danger

Make sure the scene is safe before approaching. Watch for moving traffic.

📲

Call 911 Immediately

Don't assume someone already called. Give the location clearly and stay on the line.

🙅

Don't Move Them

Unless in immediate danger, never move an injured person — hidden spinal injuries can become permanent.

🗣️

Stay With Them

Talk calmly. Tell them help is coming. Keep them still. Your presence helps.

🩸

Control Bleeding

Firm, steady pressure with clothing. Don't remove the cloth — keep pressing until help arrives.

⛑️

Leave the Helmet On

Never remove an injured person's helmet. Leave it to paramedics.

🔧 Basic Maintenance
ABC quick check — before every single ride
💨

A — Air

Tires should feel firm. Soft tires make crashes more likely.

🛑

B — Brakes

Both brakes should fully stop the wheel. Spongy = don't ride until fixed.

⛓️

C — Chain & Controls

Chain on and lubed. E-bike/scooter: battery charged, controls working.

For Parents 👨‍👩‍👧

Everything you need to help your kids ride safely on Long Island — from choosing the right helmet to understanding the latest local laws.

🛒

Choosing the Right Helmet

Look for CPSC certification. Measure head before buying. Replace after ANY crash. Replace every 3–5 years. Let your child pick the style — they'll actually wear it!

💬

How to Talk to Your Kids

Lead by example — wear your own helmet. Explain the why, not just the rule. Make it non-negotiable. Praise them when they put it on without being asked.

⚡

E-Bikes & E-Scooters on LI

Suffolk: riders must be 16+, wear helmets, stay off sidewalks and roads over 35 mph. Class 3 e-bikes banned. Fines up to $500. Nassau: Class 2/3 e-bikes and e-scooters banned on county roads.
