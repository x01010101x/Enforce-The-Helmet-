About Us 💗

We're a youth-led organization started by a 12-year-old who got fed up watching people ride without helmets. If the law exists, it should be followed. It's not that hard.

👧

Our Founder

Founder & Director · Age 12

I started Enforce the Helmet because I kept seeing kids — and adults — riding bikes, e-bikes, and scooters without helmets. It's not just dangerous, it's illegal in many places. I wanted to change that by teaching people the laws and making safety feel less boring and more real.

🎯

Our Mission

To educate kids, teens, and families about helmet laws and road safety through events, resources, and online content — making safety the cool choice, not the uncool one.

👀

Our Vision

A world where every rider — no matter their age, vehicle, or neighborhood — knows the rules and wears their helmet without being told twice.

Safety Tips 🛡️

From fitting your helmet to what to do if you witness a crash — here's everything you need to know.

⛑️ Helmet Fitting Guide
A helmet only protects you if it fits right
✌️

Two Finger Rule

You should be able to fit two fingers between your eyebrows and the bottom edge of the helmet. If you can fit more, it's too loose.

✅

The V Check

Your straps should form a "V" shape right below each ear. Adjust the side straps until they meet just under your earlobe.

👄

Chin Buckle

Buckle up snugly under your chin. You should only be able to fit ONE finger between the strap and your chin.

🚫

Loose = Dangerous

A loose helmet can spin or fly off in a crash — making it almost as dangerous as wearing none at all. Always check before you ride.



🛣️ Road Safety Rules
Know the rules before you ride
🚦

Ride With Traffic

Always ride in the same direction as cars — never against them. Riding against traffic is one of the most dangerous things a cyclist can do.

🤚

Hand Signals

  • Left turn: Left arm straight out
  • Right turn: Left arm bent up OR right arm out
  • Stopping: Left arm bent down
💡

Be Visible

Use a front white light and rear red light after dark. Wear bright or reflective clothing. Don't assume drivers can see you.

🛤️

Sidewalk vs Road

Laws vary — in many places cyclists must ride on the road, not the sidewalk. Check your local rules. Always yield to pedestrians if you do use a sidewalk.



🚨 If YOU Get In An Accident
Stay calm — here's exactly what to do
😮‍💨

Stay Calm

Take a breath. Panic makes everything worse. Assess yourself — can you move safely?

📵

Don't Move If Hurt

If you hit your head or feel neck/back pain, stay still. Moving could make a spinal injury worse. Wait for help.

📞

Call 911

If you're injured or another person is involved, call 911 immediately. Don't assume it's too minor — let the professionals decide.

📸

Document Everything

Take photos of the scene, any vehicles involved, and your injuries. Exchange contact info with anyone else involved.



🆘 If You Come Upon An Accident
You don't need to be a doctor to help — just knowing this could save a life
👀

Check For Danger First

Before you approach, make sure it's safe. Check for moving traffic, hazards, or anything that could hurt you too.

📲

Call 911 Immediately

Don't assume someone else already called. Call yourself, give the location clearly, and stay on the line if asked.

🙅

Don't Move Them

Unless they're in immediate danger (like a fire), don't move an injured person. A hidden spinal injury could become permanent.

🗣️

Stay With Them

Talk to them calmly. Tell them help is coming. Keep them awake and still. Your calm presence genuinely helps.

🩸

Control Bleeding

If there's visible bleeding, apply firm, steady pressure with a piece of clothing. Don't remove the cloth — keep pressing until help arrives.

⛑️

Leave the Helmet On

Never remove an injured person's helmet. Leave that to paramedics who know how to do it without causing further injury.



🔧 Basic Maintenance
The ABC quick check — do it before every single ride
💨

A — Air

Check your tire pressure. Tires should feel firm, not squishy. Soft tires make crashes more likely and harder to steer.

🛑

B — Brakes

Squeeze both brakes — they should feel firm and stop the wheel completely. If they feel spongy or don't fully stop, don't ride until they're fixed.

⛓️

C — Chain & Controls

For bikes: make sure the chain is on and lubricated. For e-bikes and scooters: check that the battery is charged and controls respond properly.

For Parents 👨‍👩‍👧

Everything you need to help your kids ride safely — from choosing the right helmet to understanding the laws in your area.

🛒

Choosing the Right Helmet

  • Look for CPSC certification (required by law in the US)
  • Measure your child's head circumference before buying
  • Replace after ANY crash — even if it looks fine
  • Replace every 3-5 years due to wear
  • Let your child pick the style — they're more likely to wear it!
💬

How to Talk To Your Kids About Safety

  • Lead by example — wear your own helmet
  • Explain the why, not just the rule
  • Make it non-negotiable before they ride
  • Praise them when they put it on without being asked
  • Avoid shaming — focus on protection, not punishment
⚡

E-Bikes & E-Scooters

  • Most states require riders to be 16+ for e-bikes
  • E-scooters can reach 15-20mph — much faster than a regular bike
  • Many cities ban e-scooters on sidewalks
  • A helmet is even MORE important at higher speeds
  • Check your local laws before letting your child ride one
📚
